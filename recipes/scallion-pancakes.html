Scallion Pancakes

I typical eat this as breakfast or with congee.

Ingredients

  • 250g ~2 cups + 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 160g ~2/3 cup hot water
  • cooking oil
  • 2 tbsp melted shortening*
  • 1/4 tsp ground sichuan pepper or Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 40g ~1/2 cup chopped green onions

Directions

  1. Put 250g flour into a heatproof bowl. Pour in 160g water. Stir until no more loose flour or water can be seen. Combine the mixture into a rough-looking dough.
  2. Cover the dough with cheese cloth or cling film for 15 mins.
  3. Rub a layer of oil on your work surface and the dough.
  4. With a rolling pin, flatten the dough into a thin rectangle.
  5. Mix the 2 tbsp melted shortening*, 1/4 tsp Chinese five-spice powder, and 1/2 tsp salt in a bowl
  6. Spread filling over the dough then sprinkle 40g green onions on top.
  7. From the long side, roll the dough into a rope.
  8. Cut into ~6 pieces depending on how thick you want them.
  9. This is the time to freeze the pancakes if you plan on saving them for later. Stack with pieces of parchment/wax paper and freeze.
  10. In a frying pan, heat oil over high heat until you can drop a green onion in it and it sizzles.
  11. turn heat down to medium and put in pancakes. Cover with lid and and cook for ~2mins or until bottom is brown. Flip and continue until the other side is brown.
  12. Rest the pancakes on a wire rack for a minute or two before serving (condensation will form if placed on a plate straightaway). Or don't I don't care.
  13. Serve hot.
